How Stealth Works

Stealth technology aims to make aircraft less detectable to radar, infrared, visual, and other detection methods. Key strategies include radar-absorbing materials, angular surfaces that deflect radar waves, and heat masking techniques.

Modern Applications

Examples of real-world stealth aircraft include the B-2 Spirit, F-22 Raptor, and F-35 Lightning II. These aircraft use a combination of shape, materials, and electronic warfare systems to reduce detectability.
